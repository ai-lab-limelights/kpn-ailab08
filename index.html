Why existing players should be worried

KPN has 5 million households — and still sends them a bill once a month.

Sova sits between every utility provider and the customer — and the customer never calls the provider directly again.

Women's health data, household financial data, and energy behavior data — combined — creates a profile no insurer, bank, or telco has ever had. Sova builds products on it. Privately. Everyone else just watches.

In a market where people don't trust platforms with their data, Sova is the first one they do. That trust is the moat — and it cannot be bought.

The moment Sova users start investing their household savings through the app — and teaching their kids to do the same — every bank, pension fund, and wealth manager in the Netherlands has a problem.

Once Sova is the app a family trusts with their health and their home, switching back to twelve separate apps isn't a decision — it's a loss.
